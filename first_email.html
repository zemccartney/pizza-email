HELLO, good TRIXELians...
It has been so long.
For those of you who don't know me, you can still visit my old cloud wall and check out my, at this point, congealed-grease-and-hardened-cheese works
I used to be a frequent TRIXELian, this cloud-corner's preeminent scrub knight. I sent out a weekly newspaper filled with bad puns and half-baked crust. We were friends here in the cloud. Or at least I spazzed the comments and some of you spazzed back. Some of you even rose to join me as Knights of the Brown Table.
But that was ages ago! Empty has grown the box and hard on the gut wears the beyond-borderline cold pizza remains. I speak to you now from far away. I will do what we can, as we all try to in this hustle and bustle world o' ours
Where is far away? I can't righty say...I was lamping in my home, the Pizza Plains then squirt squirt squish I was evaporated and dumped in a grand machine room.
No dankly cramped dungeon, mind you. Huge terminals all about. Interfaces to...beyond comprehension. Channels and tubes and wirings of billions of colors, pigments my mozzarella-ball optical appratus can barely process. Though it's not all so delirious. Some terminals show homely scenes from around the TRIXELian Cloud; I even sometimes glimpse my dear old Pizza Plains. And there are loads of super-comfy wheely chairs to boot. The whole place, plated in warm metal that feels like shag carpet on my threadbare pizzaFeet
Beneath the phantasmagoria and pictures of TRIXEL-life, there's the compact machine from which I transmit to you now: a simple black screen and blinking green prompt that read "Pizzaman, transmit"; and the attendant cerebroplug, freshly deglazed, cleaned, and scented. When I approached and plugged myself, a sweet disembodied voice rang overhead: "You are now connected back to TRIXEL. Chat em up if you wish". And, so, well, here we are.
I will transmit again when I can.
